Heed with Caution!!

Although urban exploration is a very fun hobby, it still has its downsides just like anything else. Below, we will cover some of the dangers of urban exploration, what to watch out for and some helpful tools you should always have with you. I highly recommend doing as much research on the place as you can, scoping it out before you go to get an idea of the necessary gear you will need to stay safe.

Never Go Alone!!!

Gear: What you should bring!

  • Navigation: A map, compass, GPS, etc
  • Sun Protection: Sunblock, glasses
  • Insulation: Clothing, rain gear
  • Light: Flashlight, headlamp, clip light
  • First Aid: Kit with any supplies for the individuals conditions (glucose supplements, inhalers, medications, etc.)
  • Fire: Waterproof matches, firestarter, lighter
  • Repair Kit & Tools: leatherman tool, utility knife, etc
  • Nutrition: Protein bars, trail mix, beef jerky, etc
  • Hydration: Water or sports drink
  • Emergency Shelter: Tent, bivvy bag, tapr shelter, etc

Hazards: Health & Safety

  • Falling from multi-story buildings due to unstable/rotted flooring
  • Inhaling dangerous fumes, gases or particles
  • Cuts, scrapes, and bruises - be sure your Tetanus shots are up-to-date!
  • Encroaching on territory of gangs, drug-users, or hostile vagrants
