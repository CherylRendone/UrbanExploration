Chernobyl Nuclear Power Plant

A very popular place is located in Pripyat, Ukraine where the Chernobyl explosion occured. The Chernobyl disaster was a nuclear accident that occured on April 26th, 1986 at the Chernobyl Nuclear Power Plant. This disaster is said to be one of only two nuclear energy disasters rated at seven which is the maximum severity on the International Nuclear Event Scale.

The accident started while there was a safety test being done on an RBMK-type nuclear reactor; the test was a simmulation of an electrical power outage to help in the development of a safety procedure for maintaining cooling water circulation until the back-up generators could provide power. The operating gap was about one minute and had been identified as a potential safety problem which could cause the nuclear reactor core to overheat. There had been three previous tests conducted since 1982, but a solution was never found. For the fourth attempt, the test had been delayed by 10 hours, so the operating shift that had been prepared for the testing was not present. Unfortunately, the testing supervisor failed to follow the procedure, creating an unstable operating condition. The unstable condition combined with inherent RBMK reactor design flaws and the intentional disabling of several nuclear reactor safety systems, resulted in an uncontrolled nuclear chain reaction. This released a large amount of enery vapourising superheated cooling water and rupturing the reactor core in a highly destructive steam explosion. This was immediately followed by the open-air reactor core fire that released a considerable amount of airborne radioactive contamination for roughly nine days before it was finally contained on May 4th, 1986.

An area known as "the zone of alienation" is largely uninhabited with the exception of about 300 residents who have refused to leave. Today, the radiation levels are still so high that the workers rebuilding the sarcophagus around the site are only allowed to work five hours per day for a month before being required to take 15 days of rest. As of 2016, 187 locals returned and were living permanently in the zone. As of 2011, Ukraine opened up the sealed zone around the Chernobyl reactor to tourists who wish to learn more about the tragedy.

Disney's River Country

Disney's River Country was the first water park at Walt Disney World. The water park is located along the shores of Bay Lake near Disney's Fort Wilderness Resort and Campground; it was themed as a rustic, old-fashioned swimming hole. The water park was opened on June 20th, 1976 and closed indefinitely on November 2nd, 2001. The water parks permanent closure was announced on January 20th, 2005, making River Country the second of only two Disney parks in the company's history to close permanently.

On March 5th, 2018, Disney filed permits to build a nature-themed deluxe resort called Reflections - A Disney Lakeside Lodge. The resort is slated to have 900 hotel rooms and is planned to open in 2022.
